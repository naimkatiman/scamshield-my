NATIONAL SCAM RESPONSE CENTRE
(NSRC)

As announced by the Prime Minister's Department on 14 October 2022, the National Scam Response Center (NSRC) has been established as an operational center to coordinate rapid response to online financial fraud. This response includes faster detection of stolen funds and enforcement action against criminals.

The NSRC is a joint venture between the National Anti-Financial Crime Centre (NFCC), the Royal Malaysian Police (PDRM), Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM), the Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission (MCMC) as well as financial institutions and the telecommunications industry. The NSRC brings together resources and expertise from all these parties to combat financial fraud more quickly and effectively.

FAQs about the National Scam Response Centre

The NSRC's focus is on online financial fraud i.e. cases where the victim realizes that money in their bank account has been transferred without their knowledge as well as cases where the victim has been tricked into transferring their money through an online financial service.

Among these fraud methods include phishing, Macau scams and malware attacks.

Victims of fraud are encouraged to contact their banks' dedicated hotline or the NSRC on 997. Please refer to Question 3 on the steps to take to get help, and Question 9 on how to contact your bank's hotline.

If you are a victim of fraud, please take the following steps to protect yourself and other victims.

  • Immediately contact your bank or 997. If you become aware of an unauthorized or unnoticed transaction in your bank account, immediately contact your bank's hotline which operates 24 hours a day (hotline bank 24/7) or call the NSRC hotline which is 997 (this line can be contacted between 8.00am and 8.00pm every day, including public holidays). You should contact your bank or NSRC 997 hotline immediately as soon as you discover that you have been defrauded. This quick action can help you reduce potential losses, although there is no guarantee that you will get your money back.
  • Make a police report. Besides reporting this incident to your bank or NSRC's hotline 997, you need to make a police report at the nearest police station as soon as possible. This action is important to allow the police to start an official investigation. To ensure immediate action is taken, you are advised to first contact your bank or the NSRC hotline before making a police report.
  • Even if the fraud incident has been going on for some time (for example more than 24 hours), you still need to report the fraud to your bank. NSRC's Hotline which is 997 is the emergency response number. Therefore, NSRC may not be able to provide a quick response in such situations as the chances of getting your money back are slim. However, your act of reporting this fraud to your bank is an important act to help the authorities take appropriate action against the criminals.

When making a report (to your bank, NSRC's hotline 997 or at the police station), you need to make sure you have the following information:

  • Brief description of fraud incident (eg chronology of events)
  • Your personal details (name, telephone number, identity card number, bank account number)
  • Suspect details (scammer) (name, phone number, date and time)
  • Details of the transaction (the number of the bank accounts involved, the amount and the time the transaction was made.
  • Transaction receipt
  • Website address (URL) shared by criminals
  • Communication with criminals (eg call logs, emails, conversations via SMS / WhatsApp / Telegram / WeChat / e-commerce / other platforms)

You also need to be prepared to provide any evidence to PDRM, for example screenshots of conversations with suspects, Facebook pages, websites, links, copies of CDM receipts

It should be emphasized that the NSRC, PDRM, MCMC, BNM or banking institutions will only contact you if you have already made a fraud incident report to them. The party will not at all ask for your personal banking information, for example username, password, PIN number, TAC or OTP.



After receiving the report, the NSRC will coordinate the response by law enforcement agencies, financial institutions and telecommunications service companies for:

  • prevent the victim from facing further financial loss;
  • try to trace the stolen funds;
  • carry out an investigation into the crime of fraud; and
  • take enforcement action against criminals.
